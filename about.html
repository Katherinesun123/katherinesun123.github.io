Hey there, I'm Kathy

Ever since I learned to read and write, I began doodling and crafting scrapbooks with my 2012 edition Teen Vogue magazine.

Choosing to be a product designer allows me to meld artistry and functionality into tangible solutions that address real-world challenges. Whether it's crafting web interfaces or mobile apps, I relish transforming abstract ideas into meticulously designed experiences that leave a lasting impact.

Let's start from the beginning. I was born in Tianjin, China but spent the developmental years of my life on the beautiful Mediterranean island of Malta. My time in this coastal sanctuary was sublime—the best years of my life even. Yet, little did I know, it was just an ephemeral respite before the rest of my life.

Once I moved to Los Angeles, I succumbed to the pressures of being the obedient pianist and ballerina Chinese daughter my single mom dreamed of. The "626" (Arcadia specifically) is famous for its night markets and boba shops but also for its relentless culture of competitive schooling and tiger parenting. It was during this era that my passion for creativity, advocacy, and the power of self-identity blossomed during the dark shadow of the time that we call 2020.

Now, I reside in the effervescent college town of Isla Vista which 15,000 UCSB students—including myself—call home. Here, I hope to explore more of life's beauties on my mint green bike with my fellow gauchos and embark on the journey that is the rest of my life.
